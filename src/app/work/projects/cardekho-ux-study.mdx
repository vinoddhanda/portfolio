## Background

CarDekho.com is one of India's leading auto portals — offering car reviews, specs, prices, and dealer listings for millions of users every month. As the platform scaled, it became critical to understand not just what users were clicking, but **why** they were visiting and what jobs they were trying to get done at each stage of their car-buying journey.

This study was a deep-dive research exercise — combining user research, competitor analysis, and structured design frameworks to define how CarDekho should serve users across every phase of the purchase funnel.

**Disciplines:** Research · Interaction Design · UI Design · Information Architecture

![CarDekho product overview](/html-2/theme/images/projects/cars-study/6-fullpage.jpg)

## The Challenge

CarDekho's challenge wasn't just search ranking — it was winning and holding user trust throughout a long, high-stakes decision process. Buying a car in India involves weeks of research, comparison, financing decisions, and dealer interactions. The site needed to serve users at every step, not just the moment of peak intent.

**Goal:** Design a highly effective user experience that matched the quality of information the platform offered — keeping users engaged from first curiosity to post-purchase.

## Research — Understanding What Users Actually Need

To understand the business structure, we first needed to understand the full spectrum of user needs. Through interviews and behavioral research, we mapped customer needs into five categories:

| Need Type | What Users Are Asking |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Emotional** | "I need a car as a gift for my wife's birthday" / "I need something for family road trips" |
| **Functional** | "Should I buy new or used?" / "What's the best car for a family of 4?" |
| **Discovery** | "What's the condition of this car?" / "What colors are available?" / "Can I get a loan?" |
| **Transaction** | "How much is the down payment?" / "What's the interest rate?" / "How much is RTO?" |
| **Post-Transaction** | "What accessories should I add?" / "Can I refinance?" / "How do I maintain it?" |

![User needs mapping in Excel](/html-2/theme/images/projects/cars-study/6-excel.jpg)

## Define — Catering to Needs Through Buying Phases

After analyzing the journeys of different user types, we worked with product managers, designers, and the tech lead to define how the platform should respond at each phase — not just to what users searched for, but to where they were emotionally and functionally in the buying process.

## Competitor Research

We ran structured analysis of what competitors were doing — not to copy, but to identify gaps and opportunities. This ongoing exercise surfaced ideas and revealed where CarDekho could differentiate on UX quality.

## Design — Wireframe Solutions

All research and ideation translated directly into wireframes. Each solution was traced back to a specific identified need or pain point — no feature was added for its own sake. The goal throughout was balancing user-friendliness with business targets.

![Wireframes — solution set 1](/html-2/theme/images/projects/cars-study/car-study-9.jpg)

![Wireframes — solution set 2](/html-2/theme/images/projects/cars-study/car-study-10.jpg)

![Wireframes — solution set 3](/html-2/theme/images/projects/cars-study/car-study-11.jpg)

Wireframes were tested with users and dealers, iterated based on feedback, and refined into final solutions before handoff to engineering.

## Reflection

The biggest surprise was how hard it was to recruit the right research participants — we had assumed access would be easy. This forced us to adapt research methods mid-project and ask sharper questions from whoever was available.

Given the wide scope, we deliberately chose to focus on the highest-impact components rather than try to solve everything at once — a discipline that kept the project moving.

## Next Steps

- Conduct further usability testing on shipped features
- Roll out remaining wireframe solutions in subsequent product cycles
- Build on the 5-needs framework as a recurring lens for roadmap prioritization
