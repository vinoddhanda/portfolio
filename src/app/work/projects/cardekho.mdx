## Overview

CarDekho (GirnarSoft) is India's largest automotive marketplace — helping millions of users every month research, compare, and buy cars. During my 7 years here (2013–2020), I worked across the full product surface: vehicle discovery, search and filter UX, comparison tools, dealer enquiry flows, finance calculators, and mobile interfaces.

This was large-scale consumer product design — where every UX decision affected millions of real users and directly impacted dealer lead revenue.

## Scale & Context

- **Millions of monthly active users** across web and mobile
- **Thousands of car variants** across 50+ brands
- **Pan-India dealer network** — enquiry flow was core business revenue
- Cross-functional teams — product managers, engineers, business stakeholders, marketing

At this scale, design decisions are not aesthetic choices — they are business decisions. Every flow change needed data justification, stakeholder alignment, and careful rollout.

## Key Work Areas

### Vehicle Discovery & Search UX
Designed the core vehicle discovery experience — how users find the right car among thousands of options:
- Search and filter UX — make, model, price range, fuel type, transmission, body type
- Homepage personalization — surfacing relevant vehicles based on user behavior
- Category pages — cars, used cars, upcoming cars, electric vehicles
- Mobile-first search experience — optimized for India's smartphone-heavy user base

### Vehicle Detail Pages
Designed the most visited page type on CarDekho — the individual vehicle detail page:
- Specs and features presentation for 100+ data points per vehicle
- Image gallery UX — exterior, interior, color variants
- Price breakdown and EMI calculator UI
- Dealer enquiry form — the primary revenue conversion point
- Expert review and user review integration
- Comparison trigger — "Add to compare" UX

### Comparison Tool
Designed the vehicle comparison experience — one of CarDekho's most used features:
- Side-by-side spec comparison for up to 3 vehicles
- Highlight differences — making complex data scannable
- Mobile-responsive comparison layout — challenging at this data density
- "Best for you" recommendation logic UX

### Dealer Enquiry Flow
The enquiry form was CarDekho's core revenue driver — dealer leads. Designed multiple iterations:
- Simplified form fields — reducing friction while capturing required data
- Multi-step vs single-step form testing
- Mobile enquiry UX — tap-friendly, fast, minimal typing
- Post-enquiry experience — confirmation, next steps, dealer contact

### Finance & EMI Tools
Designed finance calculator UI used by millions of users to estimate car affordability:
- EMI calculator with down payment, tenure, and interest rate controls
- Loan eligibility indicator
- Bank partner integration UI
- Mobile-optimized financial input UX

### Mobile App & Responsive Design
- Designed mobile-first interfaces for CarDekho's Android and iOS apps
- Responsive web design across desktop, tablet, and mobile breakpoints
- Touch-optimized interactions for India's diverse device landscape

## What I Learned at Scale

Working at CarDekho for 7 years taught me things no design course can:

**Data over intuition** — At millions of users, gut feeling is dangerous. Every design decision needed behavioral data to back it up. I developed a habit of asking "what does the data say?" before proposing any change.

**Stakeholder alignment is design work** — Getting a UX change shipped at CarDekho required aligning product, engineering, business, and marketing. I learned to present design decisions in business terms — conversion impact, revenue potential, engineering effort.

**Consistency at scale** — With dozens of product surfaces and multiple teams, design consistency required systems thinking. I contributed to CarDekho's design system — components, patterns, and guidelines that kept the product coherent across teams.

**Mobile-first for India** — India's users are mobile-first, often on mid-range Android devices with variable network conditions. Designing for this reality — not for ideal conditions — shaped my entire approach to performance and simplicity.
