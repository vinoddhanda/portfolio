## Overview

At CarDekho, every car insurance renewal required a physical inspector to visit the customer's location, photograph the vehicle, and verify its condition. This was expensive, slow, and dependent on scheduling across a large field team.

The goal was to replace this entirely — letting customers capture their own car photos through a guided mobile flow, without sacrificing the quality or legitimacy that insurers required.

## The Challenge

The existing process had multiple failure points:

- A field inspector had to physically travel to the customer location
- Scheduling required a backend team and calling team for follow-up
- Customers had no guidance on which photos were needed or how to take them correctly
- Insurance companies required **live, real-time photos** — not gallery uploads — to prevent fraud

First-round research revealed the core user problem: customers simply didn't know how many photos were needed or how to frame them correctly for an insurance-grade inspection.

## Process

### Discover

We ran usability testing with four groups: the backend scheduling team, the calling team, field runners (inspectors), and customers. This surfaced competing constraints — some insurers required live capture only, others accepted gallery uploads — and fraud prevention was a hard requirement across all of them.

![Discover phase findings](/html-2/theme/images/projects/self-inspection-6.jpg)

### Insights & Strategy

The biggest blocker was user confusion: customers didn't know what a "correct" photo looked like. Two key decisions came out of this phase:

- **Disable gallery access** — all photos must be captured live via camera to prevent fraud
- **Guide every shot** — overlay frames and instructions for each required angle so users know exactly what to capture

![Insights and strategy](/html-2/theme/images/projects/self-inspection-7.jpg)

### Prototyping & Testing

We built a prototype and tested it with real users across different device types. The step-by-step capture flow — one angle at a time, with visual guidance — dramatically reduced confusion and improved first-attempt success rates.

## Key Design Decisions

- **Brand colors and typography** carried through the entire flow to reinforce trust
- **User name, car details, and insurance information** surfaced prominently on the first screen — customers confirmed the right vehicle before capturing anything
- **Do's and don'ts tips** shown before each capture step, reducing re-capture rates
- **Gallery disabled** — live camera only, satisfying insurer fraud-prevention requirements

![Final design — step-by-step capture](/html-2/theme/images/projects/self-inspection-9.jpg)

![Final design — guidance overlays](/html-2/theme/images/projects/self-inspection-8.jpg)

## Results

Replacing field inspector visits with a self-service mobile flow eliminated scheduling overhead, reduced cost per inspection, and cut turnaround time significantly. Users who followed the guided flow completed the inspection successfully on the first attempt at a much higher rate than the previous unguided process.

## Next Steps

The next phase focused on machine learning integration: using the car's make, model, and year to generate a vehicle-specific camera frame — so the overlay matches the exact shape and dimensions of that car, making "correct framing" even more intuitive for users.
